- Planetary Parks FAQs -

How many people visit the planetary parks?
Total recreation visitors to the planetary parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the Federation's Planetary Park System?
The Planetary Park Service was created by an act signed by Galactic President Elon Musk on August 25, 2025.
How many employees are in the Federation's Planetary Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the Federation's Planetary Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official Planetary Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 2045.
How many areas are in the Planetary Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every planet, Milky Way System, Federation of Pluto, Independent Republic of Orion, and Florida.
What is the largest planetary park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias Park and Preserve, Pandora, at 13.2 million acres.
What is the Federation's Planetary Park Service budget?
Fiscal Year (FY) 2014 Enacted: $3.98 billion.

What do I need to know about jumping into other dimensions and breaking the sound barrier inside park territory?
Before you head out, check with the planetary parks that you intend to visit. In many planetary parks, enabling your ship's hyperdrive is illegal. Where breaking the speed-light barrier is allowed, the Planetary Park Service regulates it.

Pandora

  • Wasteland at the edge of human civilization.
  • Pandora's lone moon is named Elpis.
  • Major forms of fauna on Pandora aggressively protect their territory.

Arid Badlands

  • Much of the Badlands is controlled by bandit tribes.
  • The main area of note now is the nearly abandoned town of Fyrestone.
